SUBHAS CHANDRA BOSE

"Life loses half its interest if there is no struggle-if there are no risks to be taken."

Some facts about Subhas Chandra Bose and few timelines from his line:

  • Jan 23, 1897: Born in Cuttack, Orissa, India.
  • Mar 29, 1911: Bosed admitted to Presidency college.
  • Jan 1, 1916:Expelled from Presidency College in Calcutta for nationalistic activities.
  • Mar 28, 1919: He graduated from Scottish Churches College in Bachelor of Arts.
  • Jan 1, 1920: He passed the Civil service exam after completing his studies at University of Cambridge.
  • Apr 1, 1921: Resigned from the Indian Civil Service and joined Indian National Congress.
  • Dec 1, 1921: Sent to jail by the British due to his activites with the Bengal Congress Volunteers.
  • May 20, 1923: Elected leader of the All India Youth Council and secretary of the Bengal State Congress
  • Sep 14, 1925: Bose is arrested.
  • Mar 30, 1927: Bose is released from the prison and began working.
  • May 28, 1930: Bose becomes mayor of Calcutta.
  • Feb 28, 1938: Bose accepts nomination as the Indian National Congress president.
  • Oct 8, 1939: Bose organizes All India Forward Block
  • Jan 19, 1941: Bose escapes from India after he became hostile.
  • Aug 18, 1945: Subhas Chandra Bose disappears.
